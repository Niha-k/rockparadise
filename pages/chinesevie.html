Chinese calligraphy is a form of writing that uses brush strokes to create beautiful characters. The characters used in Chinese calligraphy are based on the Chinese alphabet, which consists of over 50,000 characters. Chinese calligraphy is a great way to express your feelings and emotions, as each character can have different meanings and interpretations. The brush used in Chinese calligraphy is made from a variety of materials such as bamboo, wood, and stone. The brush is used to create beautiful brush strokes, which are then used to write the characters. Each character is carefully and precisely written in order to create a beautiful piece of art. When creating a piece of calligraphy, the artist must also pay attention to the paper they are using, as this can affect the outcome of the piece. Liu Dan

Born in 1953 in Nanjing, Liu Dan is considered an important representative of contemporary Chinese culture. From a very young age, he studied Confucian classics, poetry, and calligraphy with his grandfather. As an adult, he continued to study traditional painting at Jiangsu Academy of Chinese painting. In 1981, he moved to the United States, where he stayed for 25 years. He now lives in Beijing.His work, predominantly landscapes, fuses a mix of influences: traditional Chinese techniques along with those of the masters of European art.
