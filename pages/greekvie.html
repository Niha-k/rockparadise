About Greek Calligraphy

Greek calligraphy is a unique form of calligraphy that originated in the ancient Greek culture. It is a form of handwriting that is characterized by its bold and ornate lettering. The ancient Greeks used this style of writing to express their ideas and stories in a creative and artistic way. Greek calligraphy was used in a variety of ways, including the writing of religious texts and inscriptions on stone monuments. It was also used to write letters, coins, and other documents. The style of writing was highly stylized, with the use of exaggerated curves, arches, and other flourishes to make the writing more visually appealing. The earliest examples of Greek calligraphy date back to around the 5th century BC. The Greeks developed several different scripts, including the Ionic, Attic, and Aeolic scripts. Each script had its own distinctive style, which was used by scribes to create beautiful manuscripts. The use of Greek calligraphy declined with the fall of the Greek Empire, but it was revived during the Renaissance when scholars rediscovered the works of the ancient Greeks. Irini Gonou

Irini Gonou is an artist who has worked with letters in many ways and with different materials. She was initiated to the Greek calligraphy by Agamemnon Tselikas, an inspiring professor in palaeography who helped her organise the ductus of different kind of manuscripts and historic scripts that she later reproduced in a calligraphy workshop. “Palaeography has always provided the raw material for calligraphy.” Since 2010, she has organised regular workshops and seminars on historic Mediterranean scripts and Arabic calligraphy, applying the principle of Mindful Calligraphy which “is a kind of handwriting art whose slow and deliberate strokes focus on simplicity, harmony and natural beauty aiming for a mind and body connection.” As for the Al-Mansoub style, it mainly focused on three measurements: the size of the period meaning the “Noqta”, the circle with a diameter equal to the height of the alif (the first letter in the Arabic language) and, and the height of the alif.
